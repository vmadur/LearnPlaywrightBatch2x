Kumar was a junior tester who knew only manual testing. Every day, he clicked the same buttons, filled the same forms, and checked the same pages. It was boring and slow.

One evening, his senior dev showed him Playwright. "Write a script once, and it tests everything for you," she said. Kumar was amazed.

That night, he installed Node.js and wrote his first script:

```javascript
const { chromium } = require('playwright');
const browser = await chromium.launch();
```

The browser opened by itself. Kumar gasped. He had commanded a machine.

Over weeks, he learned locators, assertions, and auto-waits. His tests ran in Chrome, Firefox, and Safari — all with one codebase. No more manual clicking.

On his first automation demo, his suite caught a regression in minutes that manual testers missed for days. His manager smiled.

From that day, Kumar wasn't just a manual tester. He was an automation engineer with Playwright.
